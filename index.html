The Cinder-Bound
  • Name
    Kano (he/him). Goliath of Fire Giant ancestry.
  • Born
    The Hotenow Cinder-Guild's illegal deep-earth tunnels beneath the volcano. Childhood measured in wheelbarrows of obsidian and coal.
  • Age
    19. Three years a freedman. Bound to the Efreeti since the geode incident at sixteen.
  • Form
    Ash-gray flesh, broad-shouldered. Eyes glow like fresh coals when his blood quickens.
  • Heat
    Body runs furnace-warm even at rest — a natural radiator. Once kept a dying lady's bones from the sea-cliff cold.
  • Voice
    Rough and halting in Common; learned to read at Emmeline's bedside. When the Rage takes him, a second voice rumbles beneath his — older, prouder, made of fire.

The Two Inside Him
  • Background
    Giant Foundling. Bound by chains, raised in heat, finally treated as a person by the woman who bought him.
  • Alignment
    Chaotic Good. He owes Emmeline a kindness he can never repay. He owes the bosses of the Cinder-Guild something else.
  • Patron
    An imprisoned Efreeti noble bound within him since the cave-in. It demands glory, power, and ash. It does not possess — it burns alongside.
  • Gear
    A dark-iron greatsword bought with seventy-five gold from a dead lady's will. Sturdy armor over ash-gray flesh.
  • Carry
    The soft memory of Lady Emmeline, who taught him his strength could be a hearth and not just a weapon.

      Kano, the Cinder-Bound

      A sleeping volcano walks the streets of Neverwinter

      To the mining bosses of the Hotenow Cinder-Guild, Kano was never a child; he was simply equipment that breathed. Born into the choked, sulfur-heavy dark of the illegal deep-earth tunnels beneath the volcano, his life was measured in wheelbarrows of obsidian and coal. Because of his size, even as a youth, Kano was forced into the "Devil's Throats" — the narrow, blistering vents where the air was thick enough to melt the lungs of human slaves. But he did not melt. While the skin of others blistered and cracked, his ash-gray flesh merely grew warm. The heat did not conquer him; it felt like a heavy, familiar blanket.

      The bosses realized his resilience and pushed him deeper, demanding he harvest the rare magmatic veins where the mountain itself screamed. It was there, during his sixteenth year, that the earth broke. A sudden, violent tremor shattered the rock face. Tons of boiling shale cascaded down, burying his crew and pinning Kano against a pulsing geode of raw, superheated plasma. He should have turned to ash. Instead, as the magma pooled around his chest, something ancient and terrible awoke in his blood. His Goliath heritage — the dormant spark of the ancient Fire Giants — flared to life, drinking in the heat.

      Through the roaring fire, a second presence made itself known. Bound within the geological prison of the geode was a noble mind of the Elemental Plane of Fire — an Efreeti of boundless pride and suffocating power. In their mutual entrapment, their spirits collided. The entity did not possess Kano; rather, it anchored itself to the roaring furnace of his lineage. When the rescue crews finally cleared the rubble days later, they did not find a corpse. They found Kano standing amidst the cooling lava, his eyes glowing like fresh coals, a low, rumbling voice that was not his own echoing from his chest. When an overseer raised his whip to drive him back to work, his control snapped. His first Rage was a storm of fire and crushing strength. He tore the heavy iron grates from the walls and shattered the primary support beams.

      The Guild was furious, seeing Kano as nothing more than a volatile, walking liability that threatened to expose their illegal slave operation to the authorities of Neverwinter. Terrified of what he had become, they drugged his water, bound him in cold-iron chains that bit deep into his skin, and smuggled him out of the mountains under the dead of night. He was liquidated quickly through Neverwinter's clandestine black market, sold off to the highest bidder to ensure he would never speak of the mines.

      · · ·

      He expected a fighting pit or a cruel lord's execution block, but in a serendipitous twist of fate, he was purchased as a bed slave by the aging Lady Emmeline, a wealthy, eccentric elderly widow who lived alone in a sprawling, drafty manor atop the fog-swept cliffs of the Sea District. With her joints crippled by severe arthritis, and the damp, salty wind of the Trackless Sea making her nights an agony of shivering cold, she purchased Kano not for his skills as a laborer, but as a hearth. Because Kano's body naturally radiated a constant, furnace-like heat, his sole duty each night was to warm the furs of her bed and lie quietly beside her, acting as a living radiator to keep her warm.

      For the first time in his life, Kano was treated as a human being. Emmeline did not chain him. She spoke to him with a soft, melodic gentleness that slowly quieted the furious, booming voice of the Efreeti in his mind. She taught him to read the common script, fed him fresh meats and sweet fruits he had never hoped to taste in the dark of the mines, and allowed him his dignity. She taught him about the giant-kin of old and listened to his rough, halting voice as he questioned her with genuine interest. In her quiet sanctuary, Kano learned that his strength did not have to be a weapon of pure destruction — it could be a shield, a comfort, a source of light.

      For three years, Kano kept the cold at bay for Lady Emmeline. But even the warmest hearth cannot stop the fading of a winter leaf. Two months ago, Emmeline passed away peacefully in her sleep, her hand resting against his arm, warm until her very last breath. Her passing threw the estate into chaos, as greedy distant relatives descended upon the manor like gulls over a carcass, looking at him with disgust and preparing to throw him back into chains.

      · · ·

      But Emmeline had anticipated them. During the reading of her testament, the family attorney revealed an ironclad clause:

      "To Kano, who kept the frost from my bones when the world grew cold, I bequeath his immediate and absolute legal freedom. Furthermore, the executors of my estate shall pay him a sum of seventy-five gold pieces from my personal treasury, so that he might clothe and arm himself to walk whatever path he chooses next."

      The relatives screamed, but the law of Neverwinter held. Kano took the gold, walked out of the estate, and never looked back. He marched straight to the city's great markets to buy a set of sturdy armor to protect him from the world, and a massive greatsword forged of dark iron — a blade heavy enough to channel the crushing strength of a giant.

      Now, Kano walks the streets of Neverwinter as a free man. He is a stranger to this sunlight, carrying the soft memory of a lady's kindness in his heart, and a sleeping volcano in his veins. The Efreeti within him stirs, demanding glory, power, and ash. The mining guilds still crawl through the dark of Mount Hotenow, unaware that their best tool is now unbound, seeking vengeance. Kano does not know where his path leads, but he knows this: the fire is his to command now, and he will burn bright.

      Neverwinter, the year of the freed cinder. A volcano walks its streets.
